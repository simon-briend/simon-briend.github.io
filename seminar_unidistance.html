About the Seminar

[The PSYR seminar] (probability and statistics young researchers seminar) is a bi-weekly meeting. The goal is to share and discover the work of young colleagues in an informal setting. We strongly encourage speakers to present accessible talks, followed by discussions and exchange. Talks are 30 minutes long, followed by questions and discussions. The aim of this seminar is to share results of young researchers, and we welcome all contributions in probability, statistics and beyond.

[When] We meet once every two weeks on Wednesday at 10am. The next talk is announced below.

[Where] Usually online. You can attend via this Zoom link.

Next talks

[Guillaume Blanc] EPFL 08/01/2025 at 10am. Invariant Poisson processes of lines/roads in the 3-regular tree.
Abstract: We consider invariant Poisson processes of lines (i.e, bi-infinite geodesics) in the 3-regular tree. In fact, there exists a unique (up to multiplicative constant) Borel measure on the space of lines that is invariant under graph automorphisms. When considering a Poisson process of lines with intensity proportional to this invariant measure, one recovers the percolation phase transition result that Benjamini, Jonasson, Schramm and Tykesson obtained in the hyperbolic plane. Furthermore, we will consider an invariant Poisson process of roads (i.e, lines with speed limits) in the 3-regular tree, and talk about the following phase transition: there is a critical value of the parameter governing the speed limits of the roads below which a.s. one can drive to infinity in finite time using the road network generated by the process, and above which this is impossible.
